Features & Traits

Amorphous

You can squeeze through a space as narrow as 1 inch wide, provided you are wearing and carrying nothing. You also have advantage on ability checks you make to initiate or escape a grapple.

Darkvision

You can see in dim light within 60 feet of yourself as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of gray.

Hold Breath

You can hold your breath for 1 hour.

Natural Resilience

You have resistance to acid and poison damage, and you have advantage on saving throws against being poisoned.

Fey Ancestry (lore reasons)

You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed, and magic can't put you to sleep.

Predator

Predator is a composite ability composed of multiple sub-abilities. It also grants spell slots to allow the casting of spells gained from Analysis.

To determine the spell slots granted by Predator, first calculate your total spellcaster level from all classes and features other than Predator using the standard multiclassing rules. Predator then contributed additional spellcaster levels equal to half the number of your remaining character levels (rounded down).

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your Predator spells. You use your Wisdom whenever a Predator spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Wisdom modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a Predator spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Predation

As an action, you attempt to engulf a creature within your reach by flowing over and partially enclosing it. The target must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or become engulfed by you.

The save DC equals 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Dexterity modifier.

You can have only one creature engulfed at a time. While a creature is engulfed, you cannot take actions other than releasing the creature. Releasing an engulfed creature requires no action.

While engulfed, the creature is partially restrained by your body. If the engulfed creature is your size or larger, its speed is reduced by 10 feet. If the engulfed creature is strictly smaller than you, its speed becomes 0. The engulfed creature can take actions normally, including attacks, spellcasting, and other abilities. It is not required to attempt to escape.

When a creature becomes engulfed, and at the start of each of your turns, the engulfed creature takes Acid damage equal to 1d6 + your Constitution modifier (increasing by 1d6 at every odd player level starting at 3rd).

The engulfed creature is not shielded from attacks. When either you or the engulfed creature takes damage from an external source, both of you take the damage. Damage is rolled once and applied to both creatures.

An engulfed creature can use its action to attempt to force itself free. The creature makes a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check, contested by your Strength (Athletics) check.
On a success, the creature escapes and moves to an unoccupied space of its choice adjacent to you.

Analysis

Whenever you kill a creature using Predation, Analysis automatically activates if it still has uses left.
At the start of play, your list of Analyzed creatures contains only Half-Elf (reason for this is explained in the lore).

Analysis has a limited number of uses, starting at 2 uses, and increasing by 1 use at 5th, 10th, 15th, and 20th player level. You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Once Analysis triggers, you cannot take actions during the turn it triggers or during your next turn.
The benefits of Analysis are not gained immediately, they only become available after you complete your next short rest (finishing the Analysis).

When you finish Analyzing a creature, the following happens:

  • The creature Analyzed becomes available to Mimicry.

  • You learn all spells the creature knows. If a spell can normally be cast only a limited number of times per day, you can instead cast it that amount of times per long rest. These spells count as known spells for you and use your normal spellcasting ability.

  • You gain all the creature’s actions, excluding legendary actions, lair actions, and actions that rely on a physical weapon. If an action has a recharge value, it retains that recharge value. If an action can be used only a limited number of times per day, you can instead use it that amount of times per long rest.
    Actions that rely on the creature’s specific anatomy (such as natural weapons, bites, claws, tails, or similar body parts) can only be used while you are mimicking that creature through Mimicry. Actions that are primarily magical or energy-based and do not require specific physical anatomy may be used regardless of your current form.

  • *You do not gain the creature’s traits, passive features, resistances, immunities, or special movement modes unless they are explicitly part of an action.

Stomach

The Stomach is a metaphysical space bound to your body. Objects you willingly absorb are stored within this space. Creatures may also be stored if willingly absorbed.

Anything stored inside the Stomach is not affected by the passage of time.

Anything stored inside the Stomach counts towards your carrying capacity, but have their effective weight reduced by a factor of 2, and increasing by 1 at 3rd, 6th, 9th, 12th, 15th, and 18th player level.

Mimicry (Recharge 5–6)

You can use your action to transform into any creature you have Analyzed. During this transformation you may grow or replace wings, but only with wings belonging to a creature you have Analyzed.

While you are transformed, the following rules apply:

  • Your movement speed is replaced by that of the Mimicked form. If you added or changed the wings of that Mimicked form, you get the flying speed of the creature the wings are from.

  • Your ability to take any action that requires hands is limited to the capabilities of the Mimicked form. Transforming doesn't break your concentration on a spell you've cast.

  • You retain the benefit of any features from your class, race, or other source and can use them if the new form is physically capable of doing so.

  • You choose whether your equipment enters the Stomach or is worn by your new form. Worn equipment functions as normal, but the DM decides whether it is practical for the new form to wear a piece of equipment, based on the creature's shape and size. Your equipment doesn't change size or shape to match the new form, and any equipment that the new form can't wear must enter the Stomach.

Isolation

Whenever you kill a creature using Predation, Isolation automatically activates. The creature’s remains are broken down into spell components that get stored inside the Stomach.
