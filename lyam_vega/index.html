Lore:

Part 1

Lyam Vega was born into an upper-middle-class family. His mother, a gentle and skilled human druid, nurtured life with her magic and heart, while his father, a brilliant but stern human artificer, harnessed arcane forces to create and innovate. However, his infernal heritage as a tiefling set him apart from the moment he was born. While his mother embraced him, recognizing the soul beneath the horns and tail, his father saw only a curse—a stain on his otherwise perfect life. Unable to reconcile his shame with his family’s reality, Lyam’s father abandoned them shortly after his birth.

Despite this, his childhood was relatively normal. The love of his mother and older sister shielded him from the harshness of the world. His sister, still too young to fully understand their father’s absence, became his closest companion. She often promised that when she grew up to be a fighter, she'd protect him from anything life could throw at them.

Part 2

Growing up in a bustling city, Lyam’s life mirrored that of any other human child—at least on the surface. He and his sister, inseparable, spent their days practicing swordplay in the woods, imagining themselves as legendary warriors. She was his inspiration and his fiercest supporter, pushing him to chase his dream of becoming a great fighter.

The prejudice against tieflings was present but muted during these years, drowned out by the comfort of his home and the strength of his familial bonds. He thrived in his little world, dreaming of proving himself one day—not just as a fighter, but as someone who could overcome the infernal shadow of his lineage.

Part 3

That dream was shattered one fateful night. A shadowy assassin, hired for reasons still unknown, broke into their home. Lyam awoke to chaos—his mother’s screams and the unmistakable clash of steel in the hall. Despite his budding skills, he was still a child, powerless to stop the rogue as they struck down his mother.

The assassin turned their blade on him, delivering a swift, brutal blow that severed his arm. As pain and shock overwhelmed him, he scrambled backward, trying desperately to escape. In his panicked retreat, the assassin cornered him and struck again, severing his leg. Weak and helpless, he had no choice but to crawl toward the only opening left to him—the door to his father’s old study, which the assassin’s strike had forced open.

Inside, amidst the cluttered remains of a forgotten life, he found salvation. An incomplete piece of arcane propulsion armor lay discarded in a dusty corner. With the assassin closing in, Lyam stretched out his remaining arm, bloodied fingers brushing against the magical apparatus.

The armor’s enchantments activated instantly, responding to his desperate touch. Arcane energy crackled through the room, fusing the armor to his broken body in a burst of light. The shockwave startled the assassin, forcing them to retreat. Though alive, Lyam collapsed unconscious, the remnants of the armor now a part of him.

When he awoke, his world had changed forever. His mother was gone, his body forever altered, and his sister—now his only family—pulled him from the ruins of their shattered home.

Part 4

The attack left scars far deeper than the ones on his body. With his mother gone and their home no longer safe, his sister found them a small, unassuming place to live, far from where their lives had been torn apart. For Lyam, returning to the old house was too dangerous—but he braved it once, sneaking back to retrieve the books from his mother’s study. Her collection of texts on druidic magic and the natural world became a quiet source of comfort in the uncertain days that followed.

To make ends meet, Lyam took a job at a local blacksmith’s shop. The blacksmith, harboring the same prejudice that plagued much of society, saw him as nothing more than a tiefling and underpaid him for his labor. However, they hired him because of the assumptions made about his arcane prosthetics, mistaking him for an artificer. While this allowed him to secure work, it left him trapped between unfair treatment and the need to keep up appearances.

In public, Lyam wears a cloak to hide his horns and tail—those physical markers of his infernal heritage that are often met with suspicion, fear, or outright hostility. The cloak offers him a small measure of privacy and protection, but it’s also a constant reminder that his presence in the world is never fully accepted.

His metallic arm and leg were both a blessing and a curse. While they allowed him to survive, they also marked him as an oddity and a reminder of his father’s abandonment. In stolen moments of quiet, he would retreat to his mother’s books, reading her words and trying to find a connection to the life he had lost.

Though he longs for the safety and happiness of his childhood, he knows deep down it’s gone forever. His sister remains his anchor, her determination giving him the strength to push forward despite the challenges they face. Yet in the quiet moments, as he pores over his mother’s writings, he begins to feel the faint stirrings of something new—a spark within, waiting to ignite like wildfire.

(Lyam quits his job at the blacksmith because he got tired of getting underpaid and treated harshly)

Features & Traits

Great Weapon Fighting

When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property for you to gain this benefit.

Egg

Your cooking skill is "HowToBasic".

You have to add 1d100 eggs to anything you cook, giving it a wild magic effect based on that number which gets triggered on consumption. You use the ingredients for the meal and up to the number rolled in eggs, requiring you to have at least a number of eggs specified by the DM in addition to the meal's ingredients.

Notes & Suggestions for the DM
Notes
Suggestions

Druid Wild Shape Uses

By default, Druids have 2 uses of Wild Shape, which only becomes infinite at level 20.
Wildfire Druids face an additional constraint because summoning the Wildfire Spirit consumes a Wild Shape use, often limiting them to one Wild Shape per encounter.

Suggested Changes (don't do multiple, might be too much):

  • a) Gain an additional Wild Shape use at each Ability Score Improvement (at levels 4, 8, 12, 16, & 19 per class)
  • b) Gain an additional Wild Shape use at each Wild Shape Improvement (at levels 4 & 8 in druid)
  • c) Gain an additional Wild Shape use upon gaining Enhanced Bond (at level 6 in wildfire druid)
  • d) Gain a separate Wild Shape use specifically for summoning the Wildfire Spirit upon gaining Enhanced Bond (at level 6 in wildfire druid)

Druid Wild Shape Forms

The restriction to Beast forms can feel limiting for roleplay and combat variety. One DM I’ve played with addressed this by allowing access to certain Monstrosities at higher levels.

Suggested Change:

  • Upon reaching level 13 (combined level across classes), you gain the ability to assume the form of specific Monstrosities, as approved by the DM.

Druid Wild Shape Max CR

By default, the maximum Challenge Rating (CR) for Wild Shape is capped at 1 at Druid level 8 (only exception is Circle of the Moon, where these suggestions could still apply). However, being able to transform into an Owlbear, like Doric in Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves, would align with the kind of flexibility and creativity that makes Wild Shape fun to use.

Suggested Changes (don't do multiple, might be too debuffed):

  • a) Allow Wild Shape forms with a higher CR at the cost of multiple Wild Shape uses.
        Example: Transforming into an Owlbear (CR 3) would cost 3 Wild Shape uses.
        Note: this change requires additional uses gained from the suggestions in the "Wild Shape Uses" part.
  • b) Allow higher CR forms but reduce their maximum duration.
        Example: For a creature with a CR higher than your maximum (e.g., an Owlbear with CR 3 when your max CR is 1), the duration is reduced by dividing the normal maximum Wild Shape duration by the creature's CR divided by your maximum CR.
        Formula: Max Duration = Normal Max Duration / (Creature CR / Max CR)
        Note: when using this the max CR using this should probably be capped to your combined level.
